    ## Why Mill Valley Drains Get Clogged
  </div>
  <div class="two-column">
    ### Redwood Roots

    Mill Valley's defining feature—those magnificent coast redwoods—is also the biggest threat to your sewer line.
    Redwood roots are aggressive moisture seekers, and your sewer lateral is an underground stream of exactly what they want.

    Roots enter through joints, hairline cracks, or deteriorated sections.
    Once inside, they expand, catch debris, and eventually block flow completely.

    **Signs of root intrusion:**
    - The same drain clogs repeatedly
    - Gurgling sounds when other fixtures run
    - Slow drainage throughout the house
    - Sewage odors in the yard
    - Unexplained wet spots or lush patches

    Our drain specialist can cut roots and restore flow, but if the pipe is compromised, roots will return.
    A camera inspection shows us the pipe's condition so we can recommend the right repair solution.

    **Tip:** If you have redwoods within 50 feet of your sewer line, annual preventive clearing is cheaper than emergency calls.
  </div>
  <div class="two-column">
    ### Century-Old Pipes

    Many Mill Valley homes—especially downtown and in the older hillside neighborhoods—have original drain lines:

    - **Cast iron** — Durable but eventually corrodes internally, creating rough surfaces that catch debris
    - **Clay tile** — Common for sewer laterals, prone to cracking and root intrusion at joints
    - **Orangeburg** — Tar paper pipes from the 1950s-70s that collapse with age

    These materials served well for decades, but they're reaching end-of-life in many homes.
    A camera inspection can reveal the condition of your pipes and help you plan.

    **Tip:** If you're buying an older Mill Valley home, a sewer camera inspection before closing can reveal expensive surprises.
  </div>

  <div class="two-column">
    ### Downtown Mill Valley: Historic Drains

    The homes around Lytton Square and along Throckmorton date from the late 1800s and early 1900s.
    Their drain systems have been working for over a century—and many are showing their age.

    Common issues in downtown homes:
    - **Undersized drain lines** that met 1900s standards but struggle with modern fixtures
    - **Multiple modifications** over decades creating awkward configurations
    - **Limited cleanout access** making clearing more difficult
    - **Shared laterals** where your drain connects to a neighbor's before reaching the main

    We work in historic Mill Valley regularly and know how to approach these systems carefully.
  </div>
  <div class="two-column">
    ### Hillside Homes: Gravity Challenges

    Homes in Cascade Canyon, Homestead Valley, and the slopes above downtown face unique drain challenges:

    **Uphill laterals:** Some hillside homes have sewer laterals that run uphill to reach the main.
    These require pump systems (grinder pumps or ejector pumps) that need maintenance and eventual replacement.

    **Long runs:** The distance from house to sewer main may be significant, with more pipe and more potential problems.

    **Difficult access:** Getting equipment to hillside homes—up stairs, through narrow passages—affects how we work.
    We're equipped for it, but it's a factor in planning and pricing.

    **Terrain movement:** Hillsides shift over time. This can stress pipes, create bellies (low spots), and open joints for root entry.
  </div>

    ## Prevention: Keeping Mill Valley Drains Clear

    **Good habits:**
    - Run hot water after using the kitchen sink to flush grease through
    - Use drain screens to catch hair and debris
    - Avoid putting fibrous foods down the disposal
    - Never pour grease down the drain—it solidifies in pipes

    **What to avoid:**
    - "Flushable" wipes—they don't break down and catch on everything
    - Chemical drain cleaners—they corrode pipes and rarely solve the real problem
    - Ignoring slow drains—they only get worse

    **For homes with redwoods nearby:**
    Annual or bi-annual preventive clearing keeps roots from becoming emergencies.
