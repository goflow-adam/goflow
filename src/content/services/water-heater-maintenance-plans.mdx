  ## Our On-Site Maintenance Process
</div>
<div class="two-column">
  ### Step 1: Understanding Your Concerns

  Our service visits start with listening. Our technician will ask if you've noticed:

  - Inconsistent or insufficient hot water
  - Strange noises (rumbling or popping)
  - Discolored or smelly water
  - Any recent changes in performance

  This helps focus the inspection and makes sure we address your specific concerns.
</div>
<div class="two-column">
  ### Step 2: Safety Inspection

  Safety is the most important part of maintenance. Your technician will check:

  - All visible plumbing connections for leaks
  - The overall condition of the unit
  - That the unit is properly installed
  - **T&P Valve** The temperature and pressure relief valve is a critical safety device.
    We test it to ensure it operates properly and confirm the discharge pipe is installed correctly.

  **For gas water heaters:**
  - Testing gas connections and inspecting fittings
  - Venting and exhaust system
  - Burner flame quality

  **For electric water heaters:**
  - Electrical connections and wiring condition
</div>
<div class="two-column">
  ### Step 3: Functional Inspection

  **Checking Heating Components**

  For gas units:
  - The burner and ignition system are inspected and cleaned if needed

  For electric units:
  - Heating elements are tested to ensure they're working properly

  **Verifying Temperature Settings**

  Your technician will check your water temperature setting and recommend adjustments
  if needed (typically around 120°F for safety and efficiency).

  **Inspecting the Expansion Tank** (if present)

  If your system has an expansion tank, it's checked to ensure it's functioning correctly
  and not waterlogged.

  **Inspecting the Tank**

  For traditional tank water heaters, we examine the outside of the tank for rust or damage
  and signs of wear that could indicate future failure.
</div>
<div class="two-column">
  ### Step 4: Maintenance Tasks

  **Flushing the Tank**

  Over time, sediment from your water and the decaying anode rod builds up inside your water heater.
  Our technician will drain the tank, flush out the sediment and debris, and refill the system.
  This improves energy efficiency, saves you money, and reduces wear and tear.

  **Inspecting the Anode Rod**

  Inside your tank is a component called an anode rod. Its job is to protect the tank from rusting.
  It intentionally wears out—and then the tank itself begins to corrode more rapidly.
  We inspect the anode rod and recommend replacement if it's significantly worn.

  **Tankless Water Heater Maintenance**

  If you have a tankless system, maintenance looks a little different.
  We descale the system to remove mineral buildup and clean internal filters.

  **Heat Pump Water Heater Maintenance**

  For heat pump units, we clean the condensate drain with solution to prevent mold and algae,
  and clean or replace the air filter as needed.
</div>
<div class="two-column">
  ### Step 5: Verification & Recommendations

  Before leaving, your technician will:

  - Restore the system to full operation
  - Run hot water to confirm performance
  - Check for leaks
  - Ensure everything is working properly

  **You'll receive:**
  - A summary of what was done
  - An overview of your system's condition
  - Any recommended repairs or future maintenance
  - Honest guidance on remaining lifespan (if applicable)
</div>

  ## Frequently Asked Questions
</div>
<div class="two-column">
  ### How often should I have my water heater serviced?

  Annual maintenance is ideal for most systems. If you have hard water or heavy usage,
  more frequent service may be beneficial. We'll advise you based on your specific situation.

  ### What's included in a maintenance visit?

  A complete safety inspection, functional testing, tank flush, anode rod inspection,
  T&P valve testing, temperature verification, and a written condition report.
  For tankless units, we include descaling. For heat pumps, we service the air filter
  and condensate drain.

  ### Will maintenance really double my water heater's lifespan?

  With proper maintenance, tank water heaters can last 15-20 years instead of the typical 8-12.
  The biggest factors are regular sediment flushing and timely anode rod replacement.
  Neglected tanks fail earlier because sediment buildup and corrosion accelerate wear.
</div>
