<article itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/Article">
<h1 itemprop="headline">Common Toilet Leak Sources and Solutions</h1>

<div itemprop="articleBody">
  <p itemprop="description">
    A leaking toilet isn't just an annoyance—it can waste hundreds of gallons of water and potentially cause serious damage to your home. Understanding where toilet leaks commonly occur can help you identify problems early and get them fixed before they become major issues.
  </p>

  <section itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowTo">
    <h2 itemprop="name">How to Identify Common Toilet Leak Sources</h2>

    <div itemprop="step" itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowToStep">
      <h3 itemprop="name">1. Base of the Toilet</h3>
      <div itemprop="text">
        <p>When water pools around the base of your toilet, it could indicate:</p>
        <ul>
          <li>Damaged wax ring seal</li>
          <li>Cracked toilet base</li>
          <li>Loose mounting bolts</li>
          <li>Condensation issues</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div itemprop="step" itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowToStep">
      <h3 itemprop="name">2. Tank-to-Bowl Connection</h3>
      <div itemprop="text">
        <p>Leaks between the tank and bowl often result from:</p>
        <ul>
          <li>Worn out rubber gasket</li>
          <li>Loose mounting bolts</li>
          <li>Cracked tank</li>
          <li>Deteriorated spud washer</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div itemprop="step" itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowToStep">
      <h3 itemprop="name">3. Supply Line</h3>
      <div itemprop="text">
        <p>Water line leaks can occur due to:</p>
        <ul>
          <li>Loose connections</li>
          <li>Worn out supply line</li>
          <li>Corroded shut-off valve</li>
          <li>Damaged compression fitting</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div itemprop="step" itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowToStep">
      <h3 itemprop="name">4. Inside the Tank</h3>
      <div itemprop="text">
        <p>Internal leaks may be caused by:</p>
        <ul>
          <li>Faulty fill valve</li>
          <li>Worn out flapper</li>
          <li>Chain problems</li>
          <li>Overflow tube issues</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/MaintenanceAndRepair">
    <h2>Warning Signs</h2>

    <div itemprop="step">
      <h3>Listen for These Sounds</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Running water when not in use</li>
        <li>Hissing or whistling noises</li>
        <li>Gurgling after flushing</li>
        <li>Periodic refilling sounds</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div itemprop="step">
      <h3>Look for These Signs</h3>
      <ol>
        <li>Water on the floor</li>
        <li>Damp or stained ceilings below</li>
        <li>Wobbling toilet base</li>
        <li>Increased water bills</li>
      </ol>
    </div>
  </section>

  <section itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowTo">

          <li>Wait 30 minutes without flushing</li>
          <li>Check if color appears in bowl</li>

  <section itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/MaintenanceAndRepair">
    <div itemprop="step" itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowToStep">
      <h3 itemprop="name">1. Supply Line Leaks</h3>
      <div itemprop="text">
        <p>The supply line connects your home's water source to the toilet's tank. Over time, these flexible hoses can develop cracks or loose connections, resulting in water pooling around the base of the toilet. A damaged supply line can often be replaced quickly, but if overlooked, it may cause significant water damage.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div itemprop="step" itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowToStep">
      <h3 itemprop="name">2. Angle Stop Leaks</h3>
      <div itemprop="text">
        <p>The angle stop, or shut-off valve, is the small valve that controls water flow to your toilet. Leaks here are typically caused by worn seals or corrosion. GoFlow Plumbing specializes in repairing or replacing faulty angle stops to restore your toilet's water control without further risk of leaks.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div itemprop="step" itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowToStep">
      <h3 itemprop="name">3. Tank-to-Bowl Seal Leaks</h3>
      <div itemprop="text">
        <p>The connection between the toilet tank and bowl relies on a rubber gasket that can wear down over time. If you notice water dripping from this area, that seal may need replacement. GoFlow Plumbing's experts can assess and replace worn seals efficiently, ensuring a secure, leak-free connection.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div itemprop="step" itemScope itemType="https://schema.org/HowToStep">
      <h3 itemprop="name">4. Base Leaks (Wax Ring Failure)</h3>
      <div itemprop="text">
        <p>A leak at the base of your toilet often points to a failing wax ring — the crucial seal that prevents water from escaping where your toilet meets the drain. This type of leak requires the toilet to be carefully removed and reseated, a process GoFlow Plumbing handles with precision and care.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
