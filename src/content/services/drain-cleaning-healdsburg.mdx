  <div class="two-column">
    ### Heritage Oaks and Root Intrusion

    Healdsburg's magnificent oak trees—some over a century old—are also the primary threat to your sewer line.
    Oak roots are aggressive moisture seekers, and your sewer lateral is an underground river of exactly what they want.

    Roots enter through pipe joints, hairline cracks, or deteriorated sections.
    Once inside, they expand, catch debris, and eventually block flow completely.

    **Warning signs:**
    - The same drain clogs repeatedly
    - Gurgling from drains when other fixtures run
    - Slow drainage throughout the house
    - Unexplained wet spots or lush grass patches in the yard

    We can cut roots and restore flow, but if the pipe is compromised, roots will return.
    A camera inspection shows us the pipe's condition so we can recommend the right long-term solution.
  </div>
  <div class="two-column">
    ### Downtown Victorian Plumbing

    The blocks surrounding Healdsburg Plaza contain beautiful Victorian and Craftsman homes—
    and plumbing systems that have been working since before World War I.

    What we typically find:
    - **Cast iron drain lines** that have corroded internally, creating rough surfaces that catch debris
    - **Clay sewer laterals** with root intrusion at every joint
    - **Undersized pipes** that met 1900s standards but struggle with modern water usage
    - **Creative configurations** from a century of modifications

    These systems can often be maintained with regular clearing, but eventually the pipe material itself becomes the problem.
    We'll tell you honestly where your system stands.

    Estate properties in Dry Creek Valley have different drain challenges than in-town homes:

    **Long sewer runs** — The distance from house to septic or sewer connection may be hundreds of feet.
    More pipe means more opportunity for problems, and clearing requires specialized equipment.

    **Septic systems** — Many Dry Creek properties are on septic rather than municipal sewer.
    Septic requires different approaches—certain clearing methods that work fine for city sewer can damage septic systems.

    **Landscape drainage** — Vineyard and garden irrigation, French drains, area drains.
    These systems need maintenance too.

    ## Keeping Healdsburg Drains Clear

    **Daily habits that help:**
    - Hot water flush after kitchen use to move grease through
    - Drain screens in showers and tubs
    - Scrape plates before rinsing—disposals aren't garbage cans
    - Never pour cooking oil or grease down drains

    **What damages pipes:**
    - "Flushable" wipes—they don't break down and catch on everything
    - Chemical drain cleaners—they corrode pipes and rarely solve the real problem
    - Ignoring slow drains—small problems become big backups

    **Tip:** If you have large oaks near your sewer line, annual preventive clearing is cheaper than emergency calls.
  </div>
  <div class="two-column">
    ## When the Problem Is the Pipe Itself

    Sometimes clearing isn't enough. We'll tell you when:

    - **Pipe is cracked or collapsed** — No amount of clearing fixes structural damage
    - **Roots return within months** — The pipe has too many entry points; repair or replacement is more cost-effective
    - **Line is bellied** — A low spot where debris accumulates requires repair
    - **Material is failing** — Orangeburg (tar paper) pipes or severely corroded cast iron need replacement

    We'd rather have an honest conversation about repair than keep clearing the same clog every season.
