    ## California Title 17: Backflow Prevention Compliance in Sonoma County

    California Administrative Code [Title 17](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/drinking_water/certlic/drinkingwater/documents/cccph/cccph_draft_feb2021.pdf) 
    requires backflow prevention wherever a cross-connection could contaminate the public water supply.
    Your local water provider enforces this law—and in Santa Rosa alone, over 10,000 devices require annual testing.
    Miss your test, and you could face fees or water service interruption.

    Here's how to stay compliant based on what's on your property:

    ### Irrigation Systems
    Dedicated irrigation lines require an **RP (Reduced Pressure) assembly** at the point of connection.
    This is the most common reason Sonoma County homeowners need backflow protection.
    Annual testing is required, and the device must be accessible for inspection.

    ### Fire Sprinkler Systems
    Residential fire sprinklers typically require a **DC (Double Check) assembly**.
    Commercial fire suppression systems require **DCDA (Double Check Detector Assembly)** configurations.
    Fire-rebuild properties in Fountaingrove, Coffey Park, and Mark West may have 
    [special requirements](https://www.srcity.org/3889/Backflow-Prevention-for-Rebuild-Areas) 
    based on when the system was installed.

    ### Wells and Auxiliary Water Sources
    If you have a well—even one that's not in active use—you need backflow protection at the meter.
    This prevents any possibility of well water entering the municipal supply.

    ### Pools, Spas, and Water Features
    Any connection to non-potable water requires protection.
    The specific device depends on the hazard level and how the system is plumbed.

    ### Boilers and Radiant Heat
    Closed-loop heating systems can create backpressure or backsiphonage conditions.
    Protection is required at the point where the system connects to potable water.

    ### Staying Compliant in Santa Rosa

    Your backflow device must be tested annually by a tester on the 
    [City's certified list](https://www.srcity.org/DocumentCenter/View/47989/Certified-Testers-Authorized-to-Test-in-Santa-Rosa-November-2025-PDF).
    The City sends reminder postcards 30 days before your test is due.
    If your device fails, you have 30 days to repair or replace it and submit a passing test.

    Contact the Water Quality/Backflow Prevention office at 707-543-3965 with questions, 
    or see the [City of Santa Rosa Backflow Prevention](https://www.srcity.org/3888/Backflow-Prevention) page.
